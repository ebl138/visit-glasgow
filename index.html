Did you know?

The Glaswegian accent (an integral part of the Glasgow patter) is undoubtedly one of the most distinct and unique things about Glasgow. Previous studies have ranked the Glaswegian accent as the most incomprehensible accent in the UK, which the typical Glaswegian (or 'weegie' to Scots outside of Glasgow), wears as a badge of honour. Common phrases include "Away an bile yer heid", which translates to English as 'Away and boil your head', meaning 'Get lost' or 'Get out of here'; "Bolt ya rocket", which means 'Run away, you idiot' and "Mad wae it", which means 'Inebriated'.

The locals of Glasgow, as previously mentioned, are affectionately termed 'weegies' by other Scots. Glasgow is home to 600,000 weegies, making it Scotland's most populous city. Famed for their charm and wit, as evidenced by the aforementioned 'Glasgow patter', its denizens earned Glasgow the 'World's Friendliest City' award in 2006. Famous weegies include Billy Connolly, Robert Carlyle, James McAvoy, Gerard Butler, Charles Rennie Mackintosh, Sir Alex Ferguson and the ancestors of British royalty (not famed for either their charm or wit).
